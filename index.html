Why Japan?

About Japan

Japan has been captivating travellers since it first opened up to international trade in 1853. While its many traditions and cultural landmarks persist, Japan is also one of the leading economic and technological centres of the world. Ancient gods and traditional customs sit side by side with cutting edge technologies and trendy pop culture, and there is always something new to experience on a visit.

Cuisine

Most loved Japanese Food

Kushikatsu (串カツ)

A Japanese dish of deep-fried skewered meat and vegetables. In Japanese, kushi (串) refers to the skewers used while katsu means a deep-fried cutlet of meat.

Meat

Beef, pork meat and cartilage, sausage, chicken parts. more.

Seafood

Shishamo smelts, wakasagi blackwater smelts, shrimps.

Vegetables

Bell peppers, lotus roots, greater burdock, pumpkins.

Sushi (すし)

A Japanese dish of prepared vinegared rice (鮨飯, sushi-meshi), usually with some sugar and salt, accompanied by a variety of ingredients (ねた, neta).

Chirashizushi (ちらし寿司, scattered sushi)

Rice served in a bowl and topped with a variety of raw fish and vegetable garnishes.

Inarizushi (稲荷寿司)

A pouch of fried tofu typically filled with sushi rice alone.

Makizushi (巻き寿司, rolled sushi)

Makizushi is generally wrapped in seaweed with cucumbers, lotus root, fish roes, tuna etc.

Ramen (ラーメン)

It consists of Chinese-style wheat noodles served in a broth; common flavors are soy sauce and miso, with typical toppings including sliced pork, dried seaweed, bamboo shoots, and scallions.

Noodles

Ramen may be thick, thin, or even ribbon-like, as well as straight or wrinkled.

Soup

Tonkotsu (豚骨, pork bone) and Torigara (鶏がら, chicken bone). The soup is broth with a typically translucent white colored appearance.

Flavors

Shio (塩, salt), Shōyu (醤油, soy sauce), Miso (味噌) and Karē (カレー, curry).

Okonomiyaki (お好み焼き)

savoury pancake dish consisting of wheat flour batter and other ingredients (mixed, or as toppings) cooked on a teppan (flat griddle).

Vegetables

Cabbage, bean sprouts, green onions.

Seafood

Octopus, squids, shrimps and oysters.

Meat

Chicken, pork belly.

Toppings

Okonomiyaki sauce, dried seaweed flakes and bonito flakes.
